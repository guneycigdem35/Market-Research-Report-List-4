<p><p>The audio monitoring market consists of two primary types: active and passive audio monitoring. Active audio monitoring involves real-time listening and assessment of sound quality, often utilizing advanced technology to detect issues instantly. This is commonly used in live events and broadcasting. In contrast, passive audio monitoring focuses on recording and reviewing audio without immediate analysis, typically employed for post-production or archival purposes. Both approaches serve crucial roles in ensuring audio fidelity across various applications, from entertainment to communication.</p></p>

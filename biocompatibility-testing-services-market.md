<p><p>Biocompatibility testing services are divided into two main categories - In Vitro and In Vivo. In Vitro testing involves using tests conducted outside of a living organism, such as cell culture studies, to assess the compatibility of a material with biological systems. On the other hand, In Vivo testing involves conducting tests within a living organism to evaluate the biological response to a material. Both types of testing are essential in ensuring the safety and efficacy of medical devices and products before they are used in clinical settings.</p></p>

<p><ul><li>Water</li><li>Clean agent</li><li>Foam</li><li>Powder</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Water, clean agent, foam, and powder are the four main types of fire suppression agents used to extinguish fires. Water is the most widely used agent due to its availability and effectiveness in cooling and smothering fires. Clean agents, like halon or CO2, are non-toxic and leave no residue, making them ideal for protecting sensitive equipment. Foam agents are effective on flammable liquid fires, forming a blanket to prevent reignition. Powder agents, such as dry chemical or ABC powder, are versatile and can be used on various types of fires.</p></p>

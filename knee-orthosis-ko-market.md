<p><p>Knee orthoses, or knee braces, can be categorized into two main types: dynamic orthotics and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are designed to provide support while allowing for controlled movement of the knee joint. They are typically used for active individuals or athletes recovering from injuries. Static orthotics, on the other hand, are designed to limit movement of the knee joint and provide stability. They are often used for individuals with chronic conditions or for post-operative rehabilitation. Both types of knee orthoses serve to support and protect the knee joint during various activities.</p></p>

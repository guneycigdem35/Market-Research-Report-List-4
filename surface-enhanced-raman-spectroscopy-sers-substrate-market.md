<p><p>Surface-Enhanced Raman Spectroscopy (SERS) substrates are materials that significantly enhance the Raman scattering signal of molecules adsorbed on their surface. These substrates typically consist of highly structured metallic nanomaterials, such as gold or silver nanoparticles, which create localized electromagnetic fields that amplify the Raman effect. SERS is utilized in various fields including biomedical diagnostics, chemical sensing, and environmental monitoring due to its high sensitivity and specificity.</p><p>The SERS substrate market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 4.28% during the forecast period. <p><p>The Surface-Enhanced Raman Spectroscopy (SERS) substrate market comprises various types, including precious metal sol, metal island membrane substrates, rough electrodes, and solid nanoparticles. Precious metal sol enhances sensitivity through the presence of nanoparticles in solution. Metal island membranes provide hotspots for amplification due to their unique morphology. Rough electrodes increase active surface area, enhancing signal strength. Solid nanoparticles, with their tunable properties, offer efficient light scattering and signal enhancement, making them crucial in analytical applications. Each type plays a vital role in advancing SERS technology.</p></p>

<p><p>Tinea Pedis, commonly known as athlete's foot, can manifest in different forms, including Interdigital and Vesicular. Interdigital tinea pedis affects the skin between the toes, causing itching and peeling. Vesicular tinea pedis, on the other hand, results in the formation of fluid-filled blisters on the feet. Both types of tinea pedis require treatment, which may include antifungal medications, topical creams, and keeping the feet clean and dry. Proper management of these conditions can help alleviate symptoms and prevent recurrence.</p></p>

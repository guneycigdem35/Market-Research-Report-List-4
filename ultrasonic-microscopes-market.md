<p><p>Ultrasonic microscopes refer to imaging devices that use ultrasonic waves to create detailed images of microscopic structures. There are two main types of ultrasonic microscopes - digital ultrasonic microscopes and scanning ultrasonic microscopes. Digital ultrasonic microscopes capture images directly in digital format, allowing for easy storage and analysis. On the other hand, scanning ultrasonic microscopes use a scanning probe to create images by measuring the interaction of ultrasonic waves with the sample. Both types of ultrasonic microscopes are used in various scientific and industrial applications for high-resolution imaging.</p></p>
